Welcome to GriefToDesign

This framework was born from profound loss but designed to create systematic hope. By walking through these questions, you'll see how personal experiences can become blueprints for preventing future tragedies.

We'll use a hypothetical scenario to demonstrate the process, but remember: this is meant to be used with real experiences when you're ready.

Question 1: What did I lose?

This question helps identify not just the immediate loss, but all the ripple effects - the future possibilities, relationships, and dreams that were also lost.

Demo Scenario:

"A parent loses their child in a preventable accident due to insufficient safety infrastructure in their community."

Immediate loss: The child's life, presence, future
Relationship losses: Parent-child bond, family dynamics, shared experiences
Future losses: Graduations, career, grandchildren, family legacy
Community loss: A contributing member, potential innovations, connections

Question 2: What caused it?

Look beyond the immediate cause to trace the longer chains of causality. Often, systemic failures create conditions where tragedies become more likely.

Causal Analysis:

Immediate cause: Accident due to unsafe infrastructure
Systemic causes:

  • ‚Ä¢ Political structures that don't prioritize prevention
Root causes: Scarcity-based governance, competition for resources, lack of cooperative planning
